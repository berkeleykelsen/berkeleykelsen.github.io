Our People

Jane Doe

Jane is a PhD student studying the intersection of legal positivism and democratic theory. She co-coordinates reading groups on early modern jurisprudence.

John Smith

John focuses on constitutional theory and the legacy of Hans Kelsen in modern Austrian thought. He also organizes workshops on global legal pluralism.

Alice Lee

Alice’s research engages with critical legal studies and theories of justice in postcolonial contexts. She is active in cross-campus collaborations.

Mark Chen

Mark studies legal hermeneutics and the influence of Roman law on early Enlightenment political theory. He is passionate about translation and archival work.

Sarah Gomez

Sarah’s interests lie in feminist jurisprudence, international law, and the ethics of adjudication. She helps coordinate speaker series and visiting lectures.

David Lin

David explores Marxist legal theory and the socio-political structures that underpin legal authority. He is involved in both academic and activist communities.
